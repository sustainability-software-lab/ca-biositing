### Executive Summary
The audit of the `mv_biomass_pricing` dataset, covering 73 records of almond-derived biomass resources, reveals significant statistical volatility and metadata gaps. The primary concern is the extreme price variance in 'almond hash' records, where observed values reach as high as 3,140 units—nearly ten times the dataset mean of 361.9. These values appear statistically decoupled from the reference mean (787.84) and suggest potential unit mismatches or data entry errors where total shipment costs may have been recorded instead of unit prices.

Furthermore, the dataset suffers from a total lack of parameter-level metadata; the `parameter_name` field is empty across all flagged records. This absence makes it impossible to distinguish between price types (e.g., F.O.B., delivered, or spot price) or to verify if moisture-adjustment was applied to the pricing. Statistical flagging is also currently sensitive due to an undersized reference dataset (n=3), which creates an artificial sense of severity for relatively consistent price clusters in the almond shells and hulls categories. Priority should be placed on validating the 'almond hash' outliers and enriching the metadata fields to ensure research utility.
