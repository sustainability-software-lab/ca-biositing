### Executive Summary
The proximate analysis dataset for agricultural biomass (n=912) exhibits high internal consistency regarding mass balance (Moisture + Total Solids ≈ 100%) but suffers from significant systemic metadata voids. Specifically, the 'analyst_name' field is 100% null, and nearly half of the records lack analyst email identifiers. This represents a critical failure in laboratory traceability and accountability protocols, even if the numeric data itself appears physically plausible.

Statistically, the majority of flagged 'LOW' severity anomalies (z-scores between 1.0 and 2.0) are driven by the inherent multi-modality of the dataset. The collection aggregates 'wet' residues (tomato pomace, grape stems, spent yeast) with 'dry' woody biomass (almond shells, cotton stems). Because the 'observed_value' mean (41.64) is a mathematical average of these two distinct populations, biologically normal values for wet residues (e.g., 80% moisture in tomato pomace) are incorrectly flagged as outliers. Future audits should segment benchmarks by resource category to reduce these false positives.
