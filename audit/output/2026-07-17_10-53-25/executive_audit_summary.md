### Executive Summary
The data quality audit of the ICP (Inductively Coupled Plasma) dataset reveals a collection of 668 records characterized by relatively high statistical variance in elemental concentrations across agricultural biomass residues. While 147 records were flagged for low-severity anomalies, many of these represent expected variance in biological materials like tomato pomace and almond hulls. However, the current dataset's maximum observed value (87,500) is more than four times higher than the reference maximum (20,560), suggesting the presence of extreme outliers or potential unit conversion errors that require validation.

A significant portion of the data exhibits negative observed values (minimum of -244.0). Per domain context, these should be interpreted as instrument noise or values falling below the detection limit (BDL) after blank subtraction, rather than critical data errors. The dataset also suffers from systematic metadata omissions, specifically the total absence of analyst names and notes for nearly all records. While legacy unit omissions are noted as non-critical, the lack of analyst attribution limits the ability to perform root-cause analysis on the more extreme statistical outliers found in the potassium (K) and sulfur (S) parameters.
